# The Last Voice on the Radio

The storm had arrived without warning.

Heavy snow fell from the dark sky, covering the forest roads within minutes. Daniel tightened his grip on the steering wheel as his truck crawled through the wilderness. He had been driving for hours, trying to reach a remote cabin he had rented for the weekend.

Then the engine died.

The headlights flickered once.

Twice.

Then darkness swallowed everything.

"Great," Daniel muttered.

His phone had no signal. The nearest town was over twenty miles away.

Looking through the snow-covered windshield, he noticed a faint light glowing between the trees.

A cabin.

Relieved, he grabbed his backpack and walked toward it.

The cabin looked abandoned. The wooden walls were rotten, and the windows were covered with frost. Yet a dim lantern glowed inside.

Daniel knocked.

No answer.

He pushed the door open.

The cabin was empty.

A fireplace burned quietly in the corner.

On a table sat an old radio.

Static hissed from its speaker.

Daniel decided to spend the night there until morning.

Hours passed.

The storm outside became stronger.

Then the radio crackled.

A voice emerged.

"Can anyone hear me?"

Daniel froze.

The voice sounded frightened.

"Hello?" Daniel answered.

For a moment there was silence.

Then the voice returned.

"Oh thank God. Someone answered."

"Who are you?" Daniel asked.

"My name is Daniel."

His blood ran cold.

"What?"

"My name is Daniel," the voice repeated. "I'm trapped in a cabin during a snowstorm."

Daniel stared at the radio.

The voice was identical to his own.

Even the breathing sounded the same.

"This isn't funny," he whispered.

The radio crackled.

"I know you're scared," the voice said. "Because I'm scared too."

Daniel stepped away.

"Who are you?"

The voice hesitated.

Then it answered.

"I'm you."

The fire suddenly popped behind him.

Daniel turned.

For a split second, he thought he saw someone standing beside the fireplace.

Watching.

When he looked again, nothing was there.

The radio continued.

"Listen carefully. You don't have much time."

"What are you talking about?"

"The thing outside is coming."

Daniel laughed nervously.

"What thing?"

The voice lowered to a whisper.

"It copies people."

The cabin became silent.

Only the storm could be heard.

Then...

Three knocks echoed from the front door.

Knock.

Knock.

Knock.

Daniel's heart stopped.

Someone was outside.

A familiar voice called through the wood.

"Daniel... open the door."

The voice was his own.

The radio screamed.

"DON'T OPEN IT!"

The knocks came again.

Harder.

"Daniel... it's freezing out here."

His hands trembled.

The radio crackled violently.

"If you let it in, you'll die."

Then the voice outside laughed.

A horrible, distorted laugh.

The lantern flickered.

The door handle slowly began to turn.

Daniel grabbed an axe leaning against the wall.

The handle stopped moving.

Silence.

Then footsteps circled the cabin.

Crunch.

Crunch.

Crunch.

Something was walking around outside.

Looking for another way in.

The radio voice spoke again.

"It's found you."

"What is it?" Daniel whispered.

The answer came softly.

"I never found out."

Suddenly, a face appeared at the window.

Daniel jumped backward.

The face belonged to him.

Same eyes.

Same beard.

Same terrified expression.

But its smile stretched impossibly wide.

The glass cracked.

The creature whispered through the window.

"Let me in."

The radio erupted with static.

Then the voice screamed one final sentence:

"YOU WERE NEVER THE REAL DANIEL!"

The radio died.

The fire went out.

Darkness consumed the cabin.

Outside, dozens of identical faces appeared in the snow, all wearing Daniel's face.

And every one of them was smiling.

The next morning, rescue workers found the cabin empty.

No footprints led away.

No signs of struggle.

Only an old radio sitting on a table.

Waiting.

Occasionally, when storms pass through the forest, travelers claim they hear a frightened voice coming through its speaker.

A voice begging for help.

A voice saying:

"Can anyone hear me?"
